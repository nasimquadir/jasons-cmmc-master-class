Exam Blueprint

The CCP exam contains 170 multiple-choice questions, lasts 3.5 hours, has a passing score of 500, and is not open book. The Blueprint weights the six domains as follows:

DomainWeightStudy Priority
1. CMMC Ecosystem5%Memorize roles
2. Code of Professional Conduct5%Ethics scenarios
3. Governance and Source Documents15%FCI, CUI, source docs
4. Model Construct and Implementation Evaluation35%Highest priority
5. Assessment Process25%CAP mastery
6. Scoping15%Scenario-heavy
Key Takeaway: Domains 4, 5, and 6 equal 75% of the exam. Master model practices, CAP phases, evidence, and scoping.
Domain 1 — 5%

CMMC Ecosystem

This domain tests whether you understand who does what in the CMMC ecosystem.

Role / EntityWhat to Know

OSCOrganization Seeking Certification.
C3PAOAuthorized assessment organization that conducts CMMC assessments.
RPORegistered Provider Organization; supports preparation, not certification assessment.
RPRegistered Practitioner; provides advice, consulting, and recommendations but does not participate in Certified CMMC Assessments.
CCPCertified CMMC Professional; may serve as assessment team member within role limitations.
CCACertified CMMC Assessor; assessment role with greater authority than CCP.
Lead AssessorLeads the assessment team and final assessment activities.
Exam Trap: Do not confuse implementers with assessors. RPs and RPOs help prepare but do not conduct certified assessments.
Domain 2 — 5%

Code of Professional Conduct

The CoPC applies to credentialed individuals, candidate credential holders, C3PAOs, RPs, RPOs, LPPs, LTPs, and other entities in the CMMC ecosystem.

Guiding Principles

  • Professionalism
  • Objectivity
  • Confidentiality
  • Proper Use of Methods
  • Information Integrity
Key Takeaway: Ethics questions are scenario-based. The correct answer usually protects objectivity, confidentiality, lawful conduct, and integrity of assessment methods.
Exam Trap: A conflict of interest does not always mean automatic disqualification, but it must be identified, documented, disclosed, and handled according to applicable requirements.
Domain 3 — 15%

Governance and Source Documents

FCI and CUI

FCI is Federal Contract Information. CUI is Controlled Unclassified Information requiring safeguarding or dissemination controls.

Emphasize: CMMC exists to protect FCI and CUI in nonfederal systems and organizations.

Source Documents to Know

  • CMMC Model Overview
  • CMMC Assessment Guide Level 1
  • CMMC Assessment Guide Level 2
  • CMMC Level 1 Scoping Guidance
  • CMMC Level 2 Scoping Guidance
  • CMMC Assessment Process
  • CMMC Glossary
  • CMMC Artifact Hashing Tool User Guide
  • NIST SP 800-171 Rev. 2
Key Takeaway: Know which document answers which kind of question. Model Overview explains structure. Assessment Guide explains practices and assessment methods. CAP explains process. Scoping Guide determines assets in scope. Glossary defines terms.
Domain 4 — 35%

CMMC Model Construct and Implementation Evaluation

This is the largest exam domain. You must understand model architecture, levels, practices, objectives, assessment methods, assessment objects, and how to evaluate implementation.

Key Takeaway: Domain 4 is not memorization only. You must apply source documents to determine whether a practice is implemented and whether evidence is adequate and sufficient.

Assessment Methods

MethodMeaningExamples
ExamineReview documents, records, configurations, policies, logs, or artifacts.Policies, procedures, screenshots, configuration files, tickets, inventories.
InterviewAsk personnel to explain how the practice is performed.System admin explains MFA enforcement; facility manager explains visitor process.
TestObserve or perform an action to verify operation.Demonstrate session lock, test access control, show alert generation.
Exam Trap: A policy alone is usually not enough. Evidence must show the practice is implemented, not merely planned.
Domain 5 — 25%

CMMC Assessment Process

The CAP is organized into four phases. CCP candidates must understand how to assist in planning, conducting, reporting, and POA&M closeout activities.

CAP PhasePurposeHigh-Yield Topics
Phase 1 — Plan and PreparePrepare assessment, validate scope, determine readiness.Assessment request, roles, documentation, scope validation, evidence approach, COI, readiness review.
Phase 2 — Conduct AssessmentCollect and evaluate evidence.Kickoff, examine evidence, conduct interviews, observe tests, score practices, validate preliminary results.
Phase 3 — Report ResultsDeliver and package recommended results.Final findings, assessment results package, CQAP review, eMASS upload, archive/dispose artifacts.
Phase 4 — Close-Out POA&MsEvaluate outstanding POA&M items.POA&M closeout, update POA&M, appeals support.
Key Takeaway: CAP questions often ask “what happens next” or “who does what during which phase.” Memorize the phase order and outputs.
Exam Trap: Do not confuse preliminary results with final findings. Preliminary results are validated before recommended final findings are created and reported.
Domain 6 — 15%

Scoping

Scoping is scenario-heavy. You must classify assets based on whether they process, store, or transmit FCI/CUI or provide security protection for assets that do.

Asset CategoryMeaningExam Focus
CUI AssetProcesses, stores, or transmits CUI.Fully in scope for Level 2.
Security Protection AssetProvides security functions or capabilities to protect CUI assets.Often forgotten in scenarios.
Contractor Risk Managed AssetCan affect security but does not process, store, or transmit CUI.Risk-managed, not assessed the same as CUI assets.
Specialized AssetIncludes OT, IoT, IIoT, test equipment, restricted systems, or government property.May have special handling and limited assessment applicability.
Out-of-Scope AssetCannot process, store, transmit, or protect CUI.Must be logically or physically separated.
ESPExternal Service Provider.Understand inherited responsibility and service relationship.
Key Takeaway: Start every scoping question with: Does it process, store, transmit, or protect CUI?
Exam Trap: Security tools that do not contain CUI may still be in scope as Security Protection Assets.

Final Cram Sheet

  1. Domains 4, 5, and 6 dominate the exam.
  2. Know Examine, Interview, and Test cold.
  3. Know CAP phase order and phase activities.
  4. Policy alone is not implementation.
  5. Scoping starts with process, store, transmit, or protect.
  6. RPs/RPOs help prepare but do not conduct certified assessments.
  7. CoPC scenarios prioritize professionalism, objectivity, confidentiality, proper methods, and information integrity.
  8. Level 2 aligns to NIST SP 800-171 requirements.
  9. Assessment evidence must be adequate and sufficient.
  10. Do not confuse preliminary results with final findings.
