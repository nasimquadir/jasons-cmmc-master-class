How to Use This Guide

Spend most of your time on Domains 4, 5, and 6. Together, they represent 75% of the CCP exam.

  1. Read the “Must Know” sections first.
  2. Memorize the tables.
  3. Use the Study Bank for weak areas.

Domain 1 — 5%

CMMC Ecosystem

MUST KNOW: Who can assess, who can prepare, who leads, and who supports. Do not confuse consultants with assessors.
Entity / RoleWhat You Need to Know for the Exam

OSCOrganization Seeking Certification. The company or legal entity seeking CMMC certification for a defined environment.
C3PAOAuthorized third-party assessment organization that performs CMMC assessments.
RPORegistered Provider Organization. Supports preparation; does not conduct certified assessments.

EXAM TRAP: RPs and RPOs are implementers/advisors. They do not conduct Certified CMMC Assessments.
Domain 2 — 5%

Code of Professional Conduct

MUST KNOW: Ethics questions usually test judgment, not definitions. Pick the answer that protects objectivity, confidentiality, lawful conduct, and integrity.
PrincipleExam Meaning
ProfessionalismRepresent your role truthfully. Do not overstate certification, authority, or affiliation.
ObjectivityAvoid actual or perceived conflicts of interest. If conflict exists, disclose and manage it.
ConfidentialityProtect customer and government data, security posture, business strategies, trade secrets, and personal information.
Proper Use of MethodsUse CMMC methods, materials, and procedures as intended. Do not improvise assessment criteria.
Information IntegrityReport results completely and honestly. Do not alter, omit, or misrepresent findings.
Respect for IPDo not misuse protected training, assessment, or customer materials.
Lawful and Ethical PracticesFollow applicable laws, contracts, NDAs, and ethical expectations.
WHAT THE EXAM LIKES: “You discover a conflict,” “client asks you to hide something,” “you saw confidential data,” “your company helped implement the controls.” The safest answer is usually disclose, protect, document, escalate appropriately, or refuse improper action.
Domain 3 — 15%

Governance and Source Documents

MUST KNOW: CMMC protects FCI and CUI. Level 1 focuses on FCI safeguarding. Level 2 focuses on CUI and aligns to NIST SP 800-171 requirements.

FCI

Federal Contract Information is information provided by or generated for the Government under contract that is not intended for public release.

Exam focus: FCI drives Level 1 safeguarding requirements.

CUI

Controlled Unclassified Information requires safeguarding or dissemination controls under law, regulation, or government-wide policy.

Exam focus: CUI drives Level 2 requirements and scoping.

Domain 4 — 35%

Model Construct and Implementation Evaluation

MOST IMPORTANT DOMAIN: You must understand practice intent, objectives, assessment methods, and evidence quality.

Assessment Methods

MethodWhat It MeansExamples
ExamineReview, inspect, observe, study, or analyze assessment objects.Policies, procedures, SSP, logs, screenshots, diagrams, tickets, configurations.
InterviewTalk with personnel to understand whether practices are implemented.Admins, managers, users, facility staff, security personnel.
TestExercise a mechanism or activity to compare expected vs actual behavior.Demonstrate MFA, session lock, alerting, access denial, scanning, logging.
MEMORIZE: Documentation shows intent. Interviews show understanding. Testing shows actual operation.

Findings

FindingMeaning
METThe practice is satisfied and evidence supports all objectives.
NOT METThe practice is not fully satisfied.
NOT APPLICABLEThe practice does not apply, and the assessor documents why.

Domain 5 — 25%

CMMC Assessment Process

MUST KNOW: CAP phase order, what happens in each phase, and what the CCP can assist with.
PhaseNameWhat HappensExam Focus
1Plan and PrepareAssessment request, roles, documents, scope validation, evidence approach, readiness review, COI handling.Assessment plan, readiness, scope, evidence validation.
2Conduct AssessmentKickoff, collect/examine evidence, interviews, observe tests, evidence gaps, score practices, validate preliminary results.Examine/interview/test, preliminary results, evidence gaps.
3Report Recommended ResultsDeliver final findings, package/archive documentation, CQAP review, upload assessment package, dispose/archive artifacts.Reporting, packaging, records, final findings.
4Close-Out POA&MsPerform POA&M closeout assessment and support appeal resolution.Outstanding items, closeout, POA&M awareness.

CCP Role by Phase

PhaseCCP Should Be Able To...
Phase 1Assist with analyzing requirements, assessment planning, evidence approach, and readiness verification.
Phase 2Assist with collecting and examining evidence, supporting interviews/tests, scoring support, and preliminary result validation.
Phase 3Assist with evidence organization, report preparation support, and final results package support.
Phase 4Understand the CCP role in POA&M closeout and outstanding issue evaluation.
EXAM TRAP: Do not confuse preliminary results with final findings. Preliminary results are validated before final recommended findings are completed.
WHAT TO MEMORIZE: Phase 1 = prepare. Phase 2 = collect/evaluate. Phase 3 = report/package. Phase 4 = POA&M closeout.
Domain 6 — 15%

Scoping

MUST KNOW: Start every scoping question with: Does the asset process, store, transmit, or protect CUI?
Asset CategoryDefinition / Exam MeaningAssessment Treatment
CUI AssetProcesses, stores, or transmits CUI.In scope and assessed against applicable practices.
Security Protection AssetProvides security functions or capabilities to the assessment scope, even if it does not handle CUI.In scope and assessed against applicable practices.
Contractor Risk Managed AssetCan but is not intended to process, store, or transmit CUI due to policies, procedures, and practices.Part of scope; SSP reviewed under CA.L2-3.12.4; limited spot checks possible.
Specialized AssetMay or may not handle CUI; includes government property, IoT, OT, restricted systems, and test equipment.Part of scope; SSP reviewed; not assessed against other practices.
Out-of-Scope AssetCannot process, store, or transmit CUI and does not protect CUI assets.Outside assessment scope; must be physically or logically separated.

Process / Store / Transmit

ActionMeaning
ProcessCUI is used by the asset: accessed, entered, edited, generated, manipulated, or printed.
StoreCUI is inactive or at rest on the asset: electronic media, memory, or physical paper.
TransmitCUI moves from one asset to another by physical or digital transport.

Common Scoping Examples

ExampleLikely CategoryWhy
Engineering laptop with CUI filesCUI AssetStores/processes CUI.
Firewall protecting CUI enclaveSecurity Protection AssetProvides security function.
SIEM used for CUI environment monitoringSecurity Protection AssetSupports CMMC security requirements.
Accounting laptop with no CUI access but on enterprise networkPotential CRMACan be risk-managed if policies/procedures prevent CUI use.
OT / PLC / CNC equipmentSpecialized AssetFalls within OT/specialized asset category.
Guest Wi-Fi fully isolated from CUI environmentOut-of-ScopeCannot process/store/transmit/protect CUI and is separated.
EXAM TRAP: “Does not store CUI” does not automatically mean out of scope. If it protects CUI assets, it may be a Security Protection Asset.

Final 800-Level Cram Sheet

  1. Domains 4, 5, and 6 are the exam. They total 75%.
  2. Know Examine, Interview, and Test.
  3. Testing demonstrates actual behavior.
  4. Documentation alone is usually not enough.
  5. Every practice must be MET or N/A for the target level.
  6. CUI Asset = process/store/transmit CUI.
  7. Security Protection Asset = protects the CMMC scope.
  8. Out-of-scope assets must be separated and must not protect CUI assets.
  9. CAP Phase 1 prepares; Phase 2 assesses; Phase 3 reports; Phase 4 closes POA&Ms.
  10. Ethics answers should preserve objectivity, confidentiality, and integrity.
  11. RPs/RPOs prepare; C3PAOs assess.
  12. CCP supports the assessment team but does not replace the Lead Assessor.
  13. Evidence must be adequate and sufficient.
  14. Preliminary results are not final findings.
  15. Use the correct source document for the question type.
